# Villages and Resources System

## Overview

This document describes how villages and resource systems operate.  
Each player manages one or more villages, each producing resources required for construction, unit training, and strategic expansion.  
Villages are the fundamental entities of the world — each with its own identity, production rates, and defenses.

## Definitions

- **Village**: A controllable entity owned by a player, capable of constructing buildings, training units, and producing resources.
- **Barbarian Village**: A neutral non‑player village that can be attacked, looted, or conquered.
- **Resources**: Core economic values used to build, train, and expand (Wood, Stone, Iron, Population, Crowns).
- **Resource Production Building**: A building that continuously produces one resource type (e.g., Wood Camp, Stone Quarry, Iron Mine).
- **Crowns**: Strategic currency generated by owned villages and used for major strategic actions.

## Core Description

Each player begins with one **starting village**.  
Villages can later be expanded through conquest of barbarian or enemy villages.

Each village maintains an independent resource stockpile and production system.  
Production is passive and continuous, scaled by the level of production buildings (Wood Camp, Stone Quarry, Iron Mine).

| Resource       | Role                                             | Produced By          |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | -------------------- |
| **Wood**       | Used for general construction and basic units    | Wood Camp            |
| **Stone**      | Used for defense and walls                       | Stone Quarry         |
| **Iron**       | Used for advanced units                          | Iron Mine            |
| **Population** | Defines workforce and limits constructions/units | Farm                 |
| **Crowns**     | Strategic currency linking economy and conquest  | Sum of village power |

Resource generation follows an exponential growth curve per level, and total storage capacity is limited by the Warehouse building.  
Villages must therefore upgrade their Warehouse to avoid production overflow.

Villages can be **attacked** and **looted**, reducing stored resources but not production speed.  
When a village is conquered, its stored resources and population are reset according to configurable parameters.

## Relationships

- The **Building System** defines the construction and upgrade of resource buildings.
- The **Population System** determines how much workforce is available to maintain production.
- The **Economy and Progression System** controls resource scaling and upgrade costs.
- The **Power System** calculates the value of a village based on buildings and army strength.
- The **Combat System** consumes resources through unit losses and repairs.

## Constraints

- Each village is an independent resource node; no shared global pool.
- Resource generation stops if storage is full.
- Villages cannot transfer resources directly (market system planned post‑MVP).
- Barbarian villages regenerate resources over time for balance.
- Crowns income depends on the cumulative power of all owned villages.
