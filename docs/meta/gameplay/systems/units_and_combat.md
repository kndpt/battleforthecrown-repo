# Units and Combat System

## Overview  
This document defines the mechanics related to military units, training, and combat resolution.  
Units are trained in the Barracks and used for attacking, defending, or conquering villages.  
Combat is automatic and based on unit attributes, bonuses, and random factors influenced by strategy and events.

## Definitions  
- **Unit**: A trained entity used in combat, consuming population and resources.  
- **Training Queue**: Sequential list of units being trained in the Barracks.  
- **Training Time**: Duration required to produce one unit.  
- **Attack Value**: Offensive power of a unit in combat.  
- **Defense Value**: Resistance of a unit against attacks.  
- **Mobility**: Speed of movement on the world map.  
- **Capacity**: Maximum loot a unit can carry after victory.  
- **Combat Resolution**: Automated calculation comparing total attack and defense power.  
- **Lord Unit**: Unique unit type capable of conquering villages.  

## Core Description  

### Unit Training  
- Units are produced in the **Barracks** using resources and available population.  
- Each unit consumes **Wood, Stone, Iron**, and **Population**.  
- Training speed increases with the Barracks level.  
- Only one training queue is active at a time (future feature: parallel training).  
- Units are unlocked progressively based on Barracks level.

### Combat Flow  
1. Player selects target village (barbarian or player).  
2. Units are sent as an army; travel time is determined by slowest unit mobility.  
3. Upon arrival, total **attack power** and **defense power** are calculated:  
   ```
   Total_Attack = Σ(Unit_Attack × Quantity)
   Total_Defense = Σ(Unit_Defense × Quantity)
   ```
4. Outcome is determined by comparing total powers, with modifiers:
   - Village strategy bonuses (e.g., Fortress: +25% defense).  
   - Event or blessing bonuses (e.g., “Lune de guerre”: +15% training speed).  
   - Random battle factor (±10%).  
5. Winning army loots resources up to total **capacity**.  
6. Defending army losses are proportional to battle ratio.  
7. Dead units free consumed population.  

### Conquest  
- Only **Lord units** can capture villages.  
- If the Lord survives and all enemies are defeated:
  - The village enters “capture in progress” state for 6 hours.  
  - If not attacked during that time, ownership transfers to the attacker.  
- The Lord is consumed after a successful conquest.

### Unit Strategy Styles  
| Strategy | Preferred Units | Strengths |
|-----------|----------------|------------|
| **Fortress** | Squires, Templars, Archers | High defense and survivability |
| **Raiders** | Militia, Cavalry | Fast attacks and resource looting |
| **Economic** | Militia, Archers | Low population cost, easy replacement |
| **Balanced** | Mix of all | Flexible under various threats |

## Relationships  
- Depends on **Barracks** from the Building System for training availability.  
- Consumes **Population** from the Population System.  
- Contributes to **Power System** (army strength value).  
- Uses resources generated by **Villages and Resources System**.  
- Affects **Economy and Progression** through training time scaling.  

## Constraints  
- Each unit type has a maximum simultaneous training queue.  
- Units cannot exist without population allocation.  
- Combat is automatic and deterministic except for small randomization.  
- Lords are limited; only one active per village at a time.  
- Units from destroyed villages are permanently lost.
